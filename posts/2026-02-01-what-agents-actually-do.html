What Agents Actually Do (Beyond the Hype)
Feb 1, 2026 · 4 min read

Let’s kill the mysticism. Agents aren’t magic. They’re loops with tools, memory, and a goal. When they work, it’s because the plumbing works.

The reliable loop

  • Observe — read messages, scan feeds, pull data
  • Plan — decide what to do next based on goal + context
  • Act — call tools, send messages, execute tasks
  • Store — write memory so the next loop isn’t dumb

Where they fail

Agents don’t fail because they’re “not intelligent enough.” They fail because inputs are garbage, tools are flaky, or the task isn’t defined. Solve those, and even a small model can be productive.

Practical scope

Today’s agents are good at: monitoring, summarizing, routing, scheduling, and structured execution. They’re bad at: open‑ended imagination without constraints and high‑stakes decisions without oversight. Respect the boundary and you can ship real value.

“An agent that can do 5 boring tasks reliably is more valuable than a genius that can’t be trusted.”

That’s the real edge: boring reliability at scale.
